The figure illustrates a hypothetical mechanism by which SARS-CoV-2 infection could potentially contribute to neurodegenerative processes.

1. **Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) Infiltration**:
   - The diagram begins with the SARS-CoV-2 virus infiltrating the central nervous system (CNS) via the BBB. This is a critical element as the integrity of the BBB is crucial for protecting the CNS from pathogens and maintaining neurological homeostasis.

2. **SARS-CoV-2 "E" Protein Interaction**:
   - Upon infiltrating the CNS, the viral envelope ("E") protein of SARS-CoV-2 interacts with TLR2 (Toll-like receptor 2) on both microglia (the immune cells of the CNS) and neurons. 
   
3. **Activation of Microglia and Neurons**:
   - **Microglia Activation**:
     - Initially resting microglia, upon interacting with the "E" protein through TLR2, become reactive microglia. Reactive microglia can lead to neuroinflammation, a state associated with various neurodegenerative diseases.
   - **Neurons**:
     - Neurons infected by SARS-CoV-2 via the same TLR2 mechanism might experience autophagy impairment and protein deposition. This is significant because dysfunctional autophagy and protein aggregation (e.g., alpha-synuclein and amyloid-beta) are hallmarks of neurodegenerative conditions such as Parkinson's disease and Alzheimer's disease, respectively.

4. **Neuroinflammation and Protein Deposition**:
   - The interaction between the SARS-CoV-2 "E" protein and TLR2 on microglia leads to their activation, resulting in a reactive state that fosters an inflammatory milieu within the CNS. Chronic neuroinflammation can cause neuronal damage.
   - Simultaneously, the same process in neurons leads to the deposition of misfolded proteins and impairment in cellular cleanup processes (autophagy), further contributing to neurodegeneration.

5. **Vascular Degeneration**:
   - Vascular degeneration is shown to result from neuroinflammation and can potentially worsen or promote neurodegenerative processes by disrupting nutrient delivery and removing waste products from the CNS.

6. **Resultant Neurodegeneration**:
   - The cumulative effects of these processes (reactive microglial inflammation, autophagy impairment, protein deposition, and vascular degeneration) impinge on the neurons' health, leading to a progressive state of neurodegeneration.
